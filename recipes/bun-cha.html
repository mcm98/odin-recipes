Bún chả

A Classic Vietnamese Dish

Bún chả is a Vietnamese dish of grilled pork and rice noodle, originating from Hanoi, Vietnam. It is exceedingly popular in north Vietnam, and I'm sure you'll like it too!

Ingredients

Dressing, Herbs and Nooodles (Bún)

  • 25g sugar
  • 15 ml rice vinegar or lemon juice
  • 175 ml water
  • 15-20 ml fish sauce
  • Garlic, chili flakes and black pepper to taste
  • 1 bunch of fresh mint, 1 bunch of fresh coriander and 1/2 cup of bean sprouts
  • 0.5 kg dried rice vermicelli noodles (Bún)

Grilled Pork (Chả)

  • 450g fatty pork shoulder and/or pork belly
  • 450g fatty ground pork
  • 50g shallots
  • 15g garlic
  • Fish sauce
  • Oyster sauce

Steps

Step 1 - Making the dressing

Place the sugar, fish sauce, water and vinegar in a small saucepan over high heat. Simmer for 2 minutes or until sugar dissolves. De-seed and finely chop chilli. Add to the saucepan with the garlic and lime juice. Stir to combine. Transfer mixture into 4 small individual sauce bowls.

Step 2 - Cooking the chả

For the patties, combine pork, garlic, egg, shallot, chopped mint, fish sauce and pepper in a bowl. Roll into 16 small balls. Flatten slightly into patties. Heat the oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Cook the patties in 2 batches for 3 minutes on each side or until golden and cooked through.

Step 3 - Cooking the bún

Place noodles in a heatproof bowl. Cover with boiling water and stand for 2 minutes or until tender. Drain well. Arrange the noodles, mint leaves, coriander, bean sprouts and patties on a serving platter. Serve alongside the 4 individual bowls of dressing.
